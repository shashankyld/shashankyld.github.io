About Me

I am a recent graduate with a Master's degree in Geodetic Engineering (Mobile Sensing and Robotics) from the University of Bonn, completed in July 2025. I also hold a B.Tech in Mechanical Engineering from IIT Delhi.

My research interests lie at the intersection of SLAM (Simultaneous Localization and Mapping), augmented reality, and world models. I am particularly interested in developing these technologies to solve real-world problems in robotics and human-computer interaction.

I have worked as a Student Assistant at both the Stachniss Lab and Agrobotics Lab at the University of Bonn, where I contributed to cutting-edge research in robotics and computer vision.

Research Interests

  • Simultaneous Localization and Mapping (SLAM)
  • Augmented Reality and Mixed Reality Systems
  • World Models and Spatial AI
  • Mobile Robotics and Autonomous Systems

  • Agricultural Robotics

Philosophy & Vision

"I have a dream to map the entire world in 3D and 4Pi."

I believe in the power of spatial understanding and the mathematical representation of world states. My work is influenced by the fundamental laws of mechanics (Igor Irodov) and philosophical thoughts on language and representation (Ludwig Wittgenstein).
