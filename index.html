Conference Papers and Awards

  • Development of an Autonomous UAV Integrated with a Manipulator and a Soft Gripper | IEEE - ASCC '22

    Debadrata Sarkar, Aman Arora, Soumen Sen, Sree SS Katta, *D Shashank, M Rohan, SK Saha

    We present a quad-rotor drone system that includes a manipulator and a soft gripper, designed for delivering small packages of up to 500 grams with care. The paper details the design and development of the system, which integrates the drone, manipulator, and gripper, and discusses potential applications in fields like industry automation, defense, agriculture, and more.

  • Aeromechanical Design and Analysis of H-Drone Configuration | IEEE - ASCC '22

    D Dwarakanathan, S Raja, P Shanmugam, *D Shashank, M Rohan, C Selvam, R Sanjeeth Kevin, Sree SS Katta, SK Saha

    We conducted collaborative research between the Indian Institute of Technology, Delhi (IITD) and CSIRNAL to design and analyze the aeromechanics of H-Drone. Our numerical simulations evaluated the aerodynamic and propulsion characteristics, as well as the structural design of the H-Drone. The H-Drone's arms were analyzed for their ability to withstand loads from aerodynamics and rotating propulsive systems. We developed a finite element model and evaluated the structural margin of safety through stress analysis. Additionally, we performed free vibration analysis to establish the structural dynamics of the H-Drone airframe.

  • 3D Pure Pursuit Guidance of Drones for Autonomous Precision Landing | IEEE - ASCC '22

    Kaushal Kishore, Sagar Dalai, Yash Jangir, Samarth Singh, M Rohan, *D Shashank, Sree SS Katta, SK Saha

    We propose a simplified pure pursuit-based method for landing a UAV precisely to its target location in the presence of external disturbance and inaccurate GPS localization. The proposed method utilizes virtual targets to generate drone way-points, which are then followed by the UAV using classical pure-pursuit. The method generates a smooth arc for the Drone to follow and avoid crashing while landing. We identify the Aruco marker placed on the landing pad using an OpenCV framework for self-localization of the drone. Additionally, a PID controller is used to ensure that the Drone follows the generated trajectory within a reliable margin of error. Our method delivers an efficient and computationally less expensive landing approach for autonomous drones.
